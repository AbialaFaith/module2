MY STORY

My journey into Front-end Web Development started when I took an ALX Professional Foundations course, which is a foundational course to learn the critical skills before transitioning into tech. My intention was to learn Data Analysis since I already had little knowledge on the subject matter. But while taking the 8-weeks course, I took a personality test that suggests tech career path that most suits you.

I took the personality test honestly and when I saw Front-end Web Development, I was indifferent: I have never thought about being a Web Developer before then. But I decided to give it a try and check it out.

The first thing I did was to go online, make research on how to get started on becoming a front-end web developer in 2024.

I found a very detailed roadmap and resources online to get started and since August 2024, it's been an amazing and exciting journey.

eHealth Africa Front-End Web Development Training

On one of the groups I belong to on WhatsApp, a link was posted to apply to be trained and mentored in different tech skills, an initiative by eHealth Africa Academy.

Checking through the link, reading the requirements and checking the tech paths availabe, I was glad I saw Front-end Web Development. I applied, and over the month, I did series of tests before being accepted as one of the eHA Academy cohort 6.

I am really grateful and excited about this opportunity because I get to learn for industry experts, I have access to mentorship and guidance, live sessions and self-paced courses.

It's been an amazing ride with eHA Academy. The courses are easy to understand and the live sessions are very interactive, timely, and interesting.

There are also series of quizzes, assignments, and projects to test our knowledge. I really love this aspect. It's not enough to watch videos and learning, being able to practise and build projects is a very important aspect of this career path.

This blog you are reading is one of the spin-off projects we are tasked with in the third module of the self-pased course.

eHealth Africa Academy has really fueled my interest in Front-End Web Development and there's no stopping me. I will keep learning and building beautiful and responsive websites.
